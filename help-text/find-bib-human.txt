find-bib — search a CSL-JSON bibliography for matching entries

Usage:
  find-bib [filters...] [--cat]

Filters (repeatable; case-insensitive substrings):
  --author TXT     Match author field text
  --year YYYY      Match derived publication year
  --doi TXT        Match DOI value
  --title TXT      Match title text
  --abstract TXT   Match abstract text

Environment:
  - BIB_JSON (default: $HOME/endnote/phd_biblio.json) — CSL-JSON library
  - BIB_FILE (used indirectly by cite2bib when --cat is present)

Output:
  - default: print matching keys (one per line)
  - --cat: pipe each key through cite2bib and emit full BibTeX entries

Dependencies:
  - jq, cite2bib (required when --cat is specified)

Workflow tips:
  - Chain multiple filters to zero in on specific works (e.g. author + title keyword).
  - Store keys in a file, then feed them into cite2md or cite2pdf for fulltext.
  - Use --cat when exporting a quick BibTeX bundle from filtered results.

Examples (list → then read one):
  find-bib --author vesper --year 2013 --title jump > keys.txt
  key=$(sed -n '1p' keys.txt)
  cite2bib "$key"
  # Or: p=$(cite2md "$key")
